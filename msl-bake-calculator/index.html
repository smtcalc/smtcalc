01 — Why Plastic Components Absorb Moisture
Most surface mount ICs use plastic encapsulation. The mold compound that protects the die is permeable to water vapor, absorbing moisture from the surrounding air slowly and invisibly. This is not a defect. It is a material property.
The problem occurs during reflow soldering, when the component is rapidly heated to 220–260°C. The absorbed moisture vaporizes and expands violently inside the package. The result is called popcorning: internal delamination and micro-cracking that may not be visible externally but causes early field failures. Pads lift, bond wires break, and the die cracks from the stress.

Floor life = how long a component may be exposed to uncontrolled factory air (30°C / 60% RH) after opening the moisture-barrier bag. Once the clock expires, baking is mandatory before reflow.
02 — The Baking Process — How It Works
1
Component absorbs moisture
Stored in open air. Moisture diffuses through the mold compound, concentrating near the die attach and paddle.
2
Oven raises temperature
125°C, 90°C, or 40°C with RH below 5%. Heat reverses the diffusion gradient: moisture migrates outward.
3
Moisture evaporates out
Water vapor escapes through the package surface. The process follows Fickian diffusion kinetics.
4
Component is dry and safe
Floor life clock resets to zero. Component may be reflowed within the rated floor life window.
⚠  Critical: all bake temperatures require less than 5% RH in the oven. A warm oven with high humidity will not dry the component. It may actually drive moisture deeper.
03 — How to Use the Calculator — Inputs
Component Parameters
1. MSL Level
Select from MSL 1 to 6. MSL 1 never needs baking. MSL 6 always needs baking before every use. Floor life is shown automatically for reference.
2. Package Body Thickness
Measure the body only; exclude leads, balls, and pins. Five bands: under 0.5 mm, 0.5–0.8 mm, 0.8–1.4 mm, 1.4–2.0 mm, and over 2.0 mm. Thicker packages need more bake time.
3. Large BGA / Stacked Die
Check this for any package larger than 17×17 mm or any stacked die component. J-STD-033D Note 2 applies: fixed 96h at 125°C regardless of thickness.
Floor Life Exposure
4. Time Out of Bag (hours)
Cumulative hours the component has been in factory air since the moisture-barrier bag was first opened. Partial days count, be precise.
5. Exposure Condition
Standard = up to 30°C / 60% RH. If factory humidity exceeded 60% RH at any point, select Elevated. This triggers a note to track desorption data instead of relying purely on the timed bake.
Auto-Classification
The calculator automatically determines whether your excess exposure exceeds 72 hours. This threshold governs which column of J-STD-033D Table 4-1 applies. The result is displayed before the bake time table.

05 — Special Cases and Practical Rules
MSL 1 — No bake ever
MSL 1 components are not moisture-sensitive. Unlimited floor life. The calculator will tell you no bake is required and will never show a bake time table.
MSL 6 — Mandatory bake
MSL 6 must be baked before every single use. There is no standard table. Bake conditions come from the component supplier datasheet. Contact the manufacturer.
Thin MSL 2 (below 1.4 mm)
J-STD-033D Table 4-1 Note 4: baking is not required for MSL 2 devices with body thickness below 1.4 mm, even after floor life exceedance. The thin geometry allows rapid re-equilibration.
Large BGA / Stacked Die
Packages over 17×17 mm or any stacked die: fixed 96 hours at 125°C per Note 2. Thickness bands do not apply. For stacked die, verify with supplier. Actual time may be longer.
Elevated Humidity Exposure
If the component was exposed above 60% RH, the tabled times may be insufficient. Track desorption by periodic weighing rather than relying solely on the time value.
The 72-Hour Threshold
Table 4-1 has two columns: excess under 72h and excess over 72h. Longer excess exposure means more moisture; the over-72h column requires meaningfully longer bake times.
06 — Quick Reference Checklist
  • Check component label for MSL level and floor life
  • Record bag-open date and time on the component tray
  • Monitor cumulative exposure, partial shifts count
  • Enter MSL level, package thickness, and hours out of bag
  • Select bake temperature available in your facility
  • Confirm oven RH is below 5% before starting the bake
  • Do not use a warm oven with high RH, it will not work
  • Do not rely on guesswork for MSL 6, always consult the datasheet

Exposure Status
--
After baking, the floor life clock resets to zero. Components may be re-exposed and re-baked up to the maximum reflow pass limit for the package. Each bake cycle degrades solderability slightly -- minimize unnecessary bakes. If a component has been baked more than the supplier recommendation, verify solderability before use.

Elevated humidity exposure noted: J-STD-033D Table 4-1 Note 1 states that if components have been exposed above 60% RH, the tabled bake times may not be sufficient. Track actual desorption data (weigh the component lot periodically during baking) to confirm dryness rather than relying solely on the timed values.
